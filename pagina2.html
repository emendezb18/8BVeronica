Cold War

12 de marzo de 1945 – 25 de diciembre de 1991

Origin

The Cold War emerged from the ideological differences between the democratic, capitalist West, led by the United States, and the communist, socialist East, led by the Soviet Union. Tensions escalated as both superpowers sought to spread their influence globally

Geographical accidents

Berlin Wall (Germany):This Wall was builder in 1961 , its was a barrier that separated the city of Berlin into two parts , one part that is the Western leaded by the United States and the other part Eastern by the Union Soviet. This wall symbolized the division between the communist east and the capitalist west and became a major conflict point during the Cold War.

Iron Curtain:Made by Winston Churchill the meaning of the Iron Curtain referred to the division physical between Eastern Europe and the democratic West.The geographical line represented by the border between the Soviet bloc and the Western powers.

Cuban Missile Crisis (Cuba):The placement of the Soviet Missiles in Cuba in 1962 brought the United States and the Soviet Union close to a nuclear war.To the geographical proximity of Cuba to the United States and increased tensions during this event of the Cold War.

Korean War:The Korean Peninsula became and critic spot of the Cold War when the forces supported by the Soviet Union and China invaded South Korea in 1950.The conflict result in a divided Korea with the north that was influenced by the communism and the South aligned with the West

Arms Race and Military Installations:United States and the Soviet Union established military bases and installations all around the world , that provoked geopolitical tensions in various regions.The crisis of the Cuban Missiles and the deployment of missions in Europe were notable examples.

Start(1945)

On July 25, 1945, British Prime Minister Winston Churchill, Soviet Premier Joseph Stalin, and American President Harry Truman met in the German town of Potsdam to discuss the fate of Germany. The meeting underlined the differences between the Allied powers, and set the stage for a cold war that would be the start in the coming decades between two global superpowers.

End(1991)

The late Cold War is characterized by a snowbreak in relations between the US and Soviet Union in the late 1980s, and is mostly associated with the figure of Mikhail Gorbachev and his perestroika reforms in the Soviet Union. In the 1980’s, Gorbachev and Reagan conducted a number of summits that led to the reduction of the two superpowers’ nuclear arsenals. In 1989, Soviet forces withdrew from Afghanistan, and the revolutionary wave in East Europe replaced communist-backed governments and Soviet allies. At the Malta summit in December 1989, Gorbachev and US President George H.W. Bush declared the end of the Cold War. The next year, the Soviet Union consented to the reunification of Germany. In 1991, the Soviet Union broke up into 15 independent states.
